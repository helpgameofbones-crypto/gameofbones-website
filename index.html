How It Happened

From frustrated dog parent
to 29 honest products

We’d been feeding our dog a popular treat for months. One day we read the label. Corn starch. BHA. Artificial colour. We put it down and never picked it up again.

Mid 2024

The kitchen experiments begin

We bought a food dehydrator. Sourced chicken from our local butcher in Kalyan. Dehydrated it slow and low. Put it in front of our dog. The reaction was instant. We knew.

Transparency

Our Process

Every step visible. No secrets. No shortcuts.

01
Sourcing
Human-Grade Ingredients Only
We source exclusively from FSSAI-certified suppliers. Every ingredient meets human consumption standards — the same quality you’d find at your butcher or fish market. Single origin, traceable, fresh. We visit suppliers personally and will not compromise on source quality regardless of cost.
🥩
02
Preparation
Hand-Prepared, Zero Chemicals
Every piece is cleaned and prepared by hand. No bleach wash. No chemical treatment. No preservative spray. We clean with plain water and prepare each batch fresh on the day it goes into the dehydrator. For fish, we debone where required. For organs, we trim excess fat for ideal texture after dehydration.
🔪
03
Dehydration
Slow-Dried at 55–70°C
This is where we differ from most commercial treats. Our dehydration runs at 55–70°C for 12–24 hours depending on the ingredient. This low, slow temperature removes moisture completely while preserving enzymes, amino acids, and micronutrients that high-heat baking destroys. The result is up to 95% nutrient retention versus around 50% for baked treats.
🌡️
04
Quality & Packing
Tested, Weighed, Sealed
Every batch is visually inspected before packing. We check for texture, colour consistency, and complete moisture removal. Treats are weighed accurately for each size. Packed in resealable, humidity-resistant zip-lock pouches designed for India’s monsoon climate. Each pouch carries a production date and best-before date.

What Can Be Returned / Refunded

Because our products are perishable food items, returns work differently from non-food products. Please read the following carefully.

✓ Eligible for Full Refund or Replacement
  • Wrong product delivered — you received a different product than what you ordered
  • Packaging damaged in transit — the pouch arrived torn, crushed or with the seal broken (photo required within 48 hours of delivery)
  • Product significantly different from description — e.g. contents do not match the labelled product at all
  • Missing items — one or more products from your order were not included in the shipment
  • Order not delivered — tracking shows delivered but you did not receive the parcel (must be reported within 7 days of the tracking delivery date)
✘ Not Eligible for Return or Refund
  • Change of mind after delivery — your dog did not like the treat, or you ordered by mistake
  • Opened or partially consumed products — for hygiene and food safety reasons, we cannot accept back any opened treat pouches
  • Incorrect storage after delivery — product quality issues resulting from improper storage (e.g. stored in a humid area without resealing)
  • Smell complaints without product defect — dehydrated meat and fish treats have a strong natural aroma. This is not a defect. The smell is a sign of quality.
  • Damage reported after 48 hours of delivery — transit damage must be reported with photos within 48 hours
  • COD orders refused at delivery — if a COD order is refused without a valid reason, we reserve the right to block future COD orders from the same address

⛔ Foods to NEVER Give

Some human foods are toxic to dogs and can cause serious health issues or death. Here's what to avoid:

🍫 Chocolate

Contains theobromine. Even small amounts cause vomiting, diarrhea, seizures, and death. Dark chocolate is more dangerous.

🧅 Onions & Garlic

Damage red blood cells causing hemolytic anemia. Both raw and cooked are dangerous.

🍇 Grapes & Raisins

Cause kidney failure. Toxicity is dose-dependent and highly dangerous.

🥛 Dairy (Milk, Cheese)

Most adult dogs are lactose intolerant. Causes diarrhea and stomach upset.

🥓 Cooked Bones

Splinter and cause choking, internal perforations, GI blockage.

🧂 Excess Salt & Spices

Causes dehydration and electrolyte imbalance.

🥗 Xylitol (Sugar-Free)

Found in gum and candy. Causes severe hypoglycemia and liver failure.

🥤 Alcohol & Caffeine

Even small amounts cause neurological damage and heart problems.

🌰 Macadamia Nuts

Cause muscle tremors, weakness, and paralysis.

🥑 Avocado

Contains persin which causes vomiting, diarrhea, and heart damage.

🍞 Moldy or Spoiled Food

Contains mycotoxins causing severe poisoning. Always check freshness.

🚨 EMERGENCY: If dog ingests any toxic food:

Contact vet IMMEDIATELY. Time is critical — most toxins are treatable if handled quickly.

🚑 First Aid Guide

🩹 Minor Cuts & Wounds

Treatment: Clean with antiseptic. Use Intas D Mag Wound Care Spray (antimicrobial spray) or Woundheal powder (healing & antiseptic). Apply sterile bandage. Watch for infection (swelling, pus, odor). See vet if bleeding won't stop.

🪲 Tick Removal

Treatment: Use tweezers to grasp close to skin. Pull straight out steadily. Don't twist. Clean with antiseptic. For prevention, use Simparica (oral flea & tick prevention). Check body daily during monsoon.

🌡️ Heat Stroke (Summer)

Treatment: Signs: excessive panting, drooling, lethargy. Move to shade. Apply cool water (NOT ice). Offer water slowly. Cool for 10-15 mins then VET IMMEDIATELY. Heat stroke is life-threatening.

💧 Dehydration

Treatment: Signs: dry gums, sunken eyes, lethargy. Offer water frequently in small amounts. Use oral rehydration salts if available. Provide shade & rest. See vet if doesn't improve in 2-3 hours.

🤢 Vomiting & Diarrhea

Treatment: Fast for 12-24 hours. Offer water only. After fasting, give bland diet (boiled rice + plain chicken). Monitor for blood. URGENT: See vet if continues beyond 24 hours or with fever.

🐛 Maggot Infestation

Treatment: Clean wound with saline. Use Simparica or Ivermectin (prescription) to kill maggots. Apply Woundheal powder. Bandage. URGENT: See vet same day — maggots spread quickly & cause severe infection.

🦴 Broken Leg / Fracture

🚨 EMERGENCY: Immobilize limb with cloth/splint. Do NOT move unnecessarily. Call ambulance/rescue organization immediately. Transport carefully on flat surface. VET SURGERY NEEDED.

🚨 When to SEE VET IMMEDIATELY

Unconsciousness • Difficulty breathing • Heavy bleeding • Suspected fracture • Severe pain • Inability to move limbs • Suspected poisoning • Maggots in wounds • Heat stroke • Persistent vomiting. Don't delay.

🛠️ Essential First Aid Kit Items

Keep these handy:

💉 Vaccination Information

⚠️ Why Feeders Should Encourage Vaccination

Rabies is 100% fatal once symptoms appear. Vaccination is the only prevention. DHPP protects against Distemper, Hepatitis, Parvo, Para-influenza — all deadly. A vaccinated dog survives; an unvaccinated one often doesn't. Vaccination protects humans AND the community stray population.

🐕 PUPPY VACCINATION SCHEDULE (Dogs)

6-8 weeks: First DHPP/9-in-1 vaccine

10-12 weeks: Second DHPP/9-in-1 vaccine

14-16 weeks: Third DHPP/9-in-1 + Rabies vaccine

Every 2-4 weeks: Deworming (until 16 weeks)

Annual: Booster shots (DHPP + Rabies)

💊 VACCINE TYPES & COSTS (Dogs)

Rabies: FREE (ABC centers), 1 dose, lasts 3 years

DHPP (4-in-1): ₹200-500, protects: Distemper, Hepatitis, Parvo, Para-influenza

9-in-1 Vaccine: ₹400-700, covers DHPP + Leptospirosis, Corona, additional strains. Better for high-disease areas

Deworming: ₹50-200, prevents worms (causes weakness, diarrhea, malnutrition)

🐱 KITTEN VACCINATION SCHEDULE (Cats)

6-8 weeks: First FVRCP vaccine

10-12 weeks: Second FVRCP vaccine

14-16 weeks: Third FVRCP + Rabies vaccine

Every 2 weeks: Deworming (until 12 weeks)

Annual: Booster FVRCP + Rabies

💊 VACCINE TYPES & COSTS (Cats)

Rabies: FREE/LOW-COST (ABC centers), lasts 3 years

FVRCP (3-in-1): ₹200-500, protects: Feline Viral Rhinotracheitis, Calicivirus, Panleukopenia

Feline Leukemia: ₹300-500, optional but recommended for outdoor/stray cats

Deworming: ₹50-200, same as dogs

💡 Pro Tip: Many municipal corporations and NGOs run FREE vaccination camps monthly. Check your local animal welfare organizations or municipal ward office for dates and locations. YODA, Karuna, and other rescue organizations often conduct free camps.

🏥 Sterilization Guide (ABC)

Why Sterilization Matters

  • Reduces population humanely — no dogs are killed
  • Eliminates breeding season aggression and roaming
  • Prevents genetic diseases and unwanted pregnancies
  • Improves behavior — dogs become calmer and less anxious

Benefits for Community

  • Fewer stray dogs = safer neighborhoods
  • Better coexistence between humans and dogs
  • Legal compliance with ABC Rules 2023 (SC mandate)
  • Healthier dogs staying in familiar territories

How to Get a Stray Sterilized (FREE)

Step 1: Identify the dog (location, color, markings)
Step 2: Contact these FREE organizations:
• YODA (Yogita Organization) — Free sterilization camps
• SOS Save Our Strays (9820141310)
• Karuna (9833625427)
• Municipal ABC Cell (contact your city)
Step 3: Schedule appointment or attend free camp
Step 4: Organization catches, sterilizes, vaccinates, releases

Post-Surgery Recovery (7-10 days)

Keep wound clean & dry • No bathing for 2 weeks • Limit activity • Soft bedding • Contact vet if wound opens

Myths vs Facts

❌ MYTH:

Sterilization makes dogs weak

✓ FACT:

Sterilized dogs are HEALTHIER, live LONGER, and have fewer health problems. They're not weak — they're stronger!

❌ MYTH:

It's cruel to sterilize animals

✓ FACT:

Sterilization is HUMANE. It prevents hundreds of puppies born to starvation & death. It's the most ethical population control.

❌ MYTH:

Surgery is dangerous; many dogs die

✓ FACT:

Modern sterilization is SAFE with >99% survival rate. More dogs die from untreated disease than from surgery.

❌ MYTH:

Free surgery means poor quality

✓ FACT:

FREE ABC camps are performed by qualified veterinarians. Quality = Paid. Safety & care are the same.

🤕 Injured Animal Guide

Broken Leg or Fracture

Signs: Severe lameness, inability to bear weight, swelling, deformity. Action: Immobilize limb gently. Keep calm and minimize movement. Get to vet immediately — spinal injury may also be present. 🚨 EMERGENCY

Hit by Vehicle

Signs: Severe injuries, difficulty breathing, inability to move. Action: Keep very calm. Minimize movement to avoid spinal damage. Rush to vet immediately. Even if appears okay, internal injuries may be present. 🚨 CRITICAL

Heavy Bleeding

Signs: Blood flowing continuously, wound won't stop, pale gums. Action: Apply firm pressure with clean cloth for 10-15 minutes. Don't check the wound. Get to vet immediately — may need transfusion. 🚨 CRITICAL

Maggot Wound Infestation

Signs: Visible white larvae in wound, foul smell, pain. Action: Do NOT attempt removal yourself. Apply neem oil. Cover with cloth. Get to vet immediately — they have proper tools. 🚨 URGENT (24 hrs)

Suspected Poisoning

Signs: Sudden behavioral change, vomiting, tremors, difficulty breathing, collapse. Action: If possible, identify what was eaten. Keep dog calm. GET TO VET IMMEDIATELY — poisoning is time-critical. 🚨 CRITICAL

Emergency Contacts: Municipal ABC Cell (check city website) • Police for cruelty cases (100) • Local Animal NGOs

☠️ Poisoning Guide: Common Indian Hazards

🐀 Rat Poison (Most Common)

Signs: Lethargy, vomiting, bleeding from gums/nose, pale gums, difficulty breathing.

Action: RUSH TO VET IMMEDIATELY — Treatable with blood transfusion & vitamin K if caught early (within 2-3 hours).

☠️ Pesticides & Insecticides

Signs: Excessive drooling, tremors, difficulty breathing, seizures, collapse.

Action: EMERGENCY — Move to fresh air immediately. Get to vet NOW. Some have antidotes if given early.

🍫 Chocolate Toxicity

Signs: Vomiting, diarrhea, restlessness, rapid heartbeat, tremors.

Action: Dark chocolate = more toxic. Get to vet. Activated charcoal may help if given within 1 hour.

💊 Human Medicines

Signs: Vary (NSAIDs cause vomiting; sleeping pills cause drowsiness/coma).

Action: Bring medicine bottle to vet. Many human drugs are TOXIC to dogs.

🚨 IMMEDIATE ACTIONS TO SLOW POISON (Do While Going to Vet)

  1. Activated Charcoal (if available): Gives ₹50-100 tablets to absorb poison in stomach. Works best within 1-2 hours of ingestion. Do NOT use for corrosive poisons (rat poison, oils).
  2. Make dog vomit (ONLY for certain poisons): Give 3% hydrogen peroxide (1 tsp per 5kg body weight) to induce vomiting if within 30 mins. NOT for corrosive substances. Ask vet by phone first.
  3. Keep calm: Stress speeds up poison absorption. Keep dog quiet, cool, and rested.
  4. DO NOT make dog vomit for: Rat poison, acids, oils, petroleum, strong chemicals.
  5. Identify the poison: Bring container/sample to vet. This helps them treat faster.
  6. Note timing: When was poison eaten? VET NEEDS THIS.
  7. GET TO VET IMMEDIATELY: These steps only BUY TIME. Professional treatment is critical.

⚠️ IMPORTANT FACTS

  • Time is life: Poisoning is time-critical. Every minute counts. Get to vet IMMEDIATELY.
  • Most poisonings ARE treatable: If caught within 1-4 hours, survival rate is high.
  • Deliberately poisoning dogs is a CRIME: Under Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act, punishment is up to 2 years imprisonment + ₹5000 fine. Report to police (100) and animal NGOs.
  • Prevention: Never leave rat poison/pesticides in open. Secure all medicines. Watch what strays eat.

🦷 Bite Prevention: Safe Approach

Understanding Dog Body Language

⚠️ Warning Signs (Back Away)

Stiff body • Raised hackles • Intense stare • Bared teeth • Growling • Ears back/forward aggressively

✓ Friendly Signs (Okay to Approach)

Relaxed body • Soft eyes • Wagging tail • Play bow • Slow approach • Relaxed mouth

Safe Approach Techniques

  1. Never approach suddenly — come slowly from the side
  2. Speak softly — intense staring is a threat
  3. Let dog approach you — don't reach out first
  4. Sit or crouch down — appear smaller
  5. Offer food as incentive
  6. NEVER pet while eating — HIGHEST bite risk

If You're Bitten or Scratched

  1. Wash immediately with soap and water for 5-15 minutes
  2. Do NOT apply home remedies — get medical care
  3. Go to hospital within 24 hours (ideally within 48 hours)
  4. Tell doctor it was a street dog — get rabies prophylaxis
  5. Complete all anti-rabies doses (usually 5 doses over 2 weeks)
  6. Report incident to municipal authorities

⚠️ CRITICAL:

Rabies is 100% fatal once symptoms appear. Vaccination is 100% effective if started before symptoms.

Trust Building Techniques

  • Consistency is KEY: Same time, place, person
  • Patience: Never force interaction
  • Soft Voice: Calm tone, avoid loud noises
  • Let Dog Approach: Don't reach out first
  • Reward Approach: Food/praise when dog comes closer
  • Sit Down: Appear smaller and less threatening
  • Play Nearby: Show friendliness through play
  • No Sudden Movements: Slow, deliberate actions only
  • First Petting: Only when eating or relaxed, chest/shoulders only
  • Read Signals: If stiffens/backs away — STOP immediately

🍽️ Responsible Feeding Etiquette

🧹 Clean Up Leftovers

Remove uneaten food after 20-30 minutes. Old food attracts pests and spreads disease. Keep area hygienic — reduces complaints and disease transmission.

🚫 Don't Block Entrances

Feed away from apartment entrances. Don't create obstacles for residents. Choose peripheral locations where dogs can eat safely and people can pass freely.

👥 Respect Local Residents

Be mindful of neighborhood concerns. Feed at less visible times. Communicate about your work. Build support, not conflict.

⏰ Consistent Times

Feed at EXACT same time daily. Dogs remember and expect. Consistency creates routine and helps track health patterns.

♻️ Dispose Waste Responsibly

Properly dispose packaging and uneaten food. Don't leave waste in communal areas. Prevents pest attraction and disease spread.

📍 Portion Size

Give 1-2 cups per medium dog. Adjust based on body condition. Fresh water always available. More is not better.

Best Practices Summary

  • Always wear gloves when handling food and dogs
  • Wash hands thoroughly (20+ seconds) after feeding
  • Use separate utensils/bowls for dog food
  • Keep your own pet vaccinated (rabies, DHPP) — your protection
  • Monitor dogs for health changes and report to vets
  • Build relationships with other feeders in your area

⚖️ BNS 2023 SECTIONS THAT PROTECT YOU - DETAILED LAWS

These are the EXACT laws protecting you from harassment. Know them!

📜 Section 129: Criminal Intimidation

THE EXACT LAW: "Whoever threatens another with injury to his person, reputation or property, or to the person or reputation of anyone in whom that person is interested, with intent to cause alarm to that person, or to cause that person to do any act which he is not legally bound to do, or to omit to do any act which that person is legally entitled to do, as the means of avoiding the threat, commits criminal intimidation."

APPLIES TO YOU WHEN: Someone threatens you (verbally or in writing) because you feed animals

📜 Section 130: Punishment for Criminal Intimidation

THE EXACT LAW: "Whoever commits criminal intimidation shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to two years, or with fine which may extend to five hundred rupees, or with both."

PUNISHMENT: Up to 2 years in jail OR fine up to ₹500 OR both

📜 Section 133: Causing Hurt

THE EXACT LAW: "Whoever intentionally causes hurt to any person by any act which he knows is likely to cause hurt to that person, shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to six months, or with fine which may extend to two hundred and fifty rupees."

APPLIES TO YOU WHEN: Someone physically hurts or assaults you

📜 Section 189: Unlawful Assembly

THE EXACT LAW: "An assembly of five or more persons is designated an 'unlawful assembly' if the common object of the persons composing that assembly is to commit an offence, or if...the persons assembled make use of criminal force."

APPLIES TO YOU WHEN: A group of 5+ people gather to threaten, harm, or intimidate you because you feed animals

📜 Section 191: Criminal Force

THE EXACT LAW: "Whoever intentionally uses force to any person, without that person's consent, in order to the committing of any offence, or intending by doing so to cause or knowing it to be likely that he will thereby cause injury to that person, commits an offence which is designated 'criminal force'."

APPLIES TO YOU WHEN: Someone uses physical violence (hitting, pushing, shoving) against you

📜 Section 351-352: Criminal Intimidation with Threat

THE EXACT LAW: "Threatening body or limb, property or reputation - Whoever makes any gesture, conveyance or word likely to be taken as an assurance of intended harm to the person, reputation or property of that person or any other, shall be punished with imprisonment...or fine...or both."

APPLIES TO YOU WHEN: Someone makes explicit threats (by gesture, word, message) against you

💡 REMEMBER:

These laws are designed to protect YOU. When filing an FIR, mention the specific section(s) that apply to your case. THE LAW IS ON YOUR SIDE WHEN YOU FEED COMMUNITY ANIMALS.

⚔️ Animal Protection Laws in India

Know the laws that protect animals and provide remedies when animals are harmed or abused. These laws make it illegal to cause suffering to any animal.

📜 PREVENTION OF CRUELTY TO ANIMALS ACT, 1960

Section 11(1) - WHAT IS CONSIDERED ANIMAL CRUELTY?

The following acts are defined as cruelty to animals and are ILLEGAL:

🚫 Beating or Over-riding

Beating, kicking, whipping, or driving an animal in a way that causes unnecessary pain

🚫 Using Animal When Unfit

Making a sick, injured, or weak animal work or carry loads

🚫 Confining Without Space

Keeping animal in confined cage/space without room to move (except medical treatment)

🚫 Withholding Food/Water

Deliberately denying food, water, or shelter to an animal

🚫 Poisoning or Torture

Poisoning, torturing, or causing deliberate suffering to any animal

🚫 Animal Fighting/Betting

Making animals fight or placing bets on animal fights

🚫 Abandoning Animals

Abandoning an animal without food, water, or shelter

🚫 Performing Surgery Wrongly

Performing surgery or medical treatment on animals without veterinary qualification

⚖️ PUNISHMENT FOR ANIMAL CRUELTY:

First Offence: Fine of ₹50-250 OR imprisonment up to 3 months
Second/Subsequent Offence: Fine of ₹100-500 OR imprisonment up to 3 months

🤝 Community Animal Coexistence Guidelines

Guidelines for peaceful coexistence. Built on compassion, responsibility, and legal compliance.

1️⃣ RIGHT TO COEXIST

Community animals have the right to live in territories. Cannot be moved or poisoned except for sterilization/medical treatment.

2️⃣ DESIGNATED FEEDING

Only at approved spots. Fixed feeding times. Avoid blocking entrances, staircases, or play areas.

3️⃣ HYGIENE & CLEANLINESS

Feeders maintain cleanliness. Remove leftover food. Proper waste disposal. Use hygienic containers.

4️⃣ MANAGING COMMITTEE ROLE

Identify feeding zones. No harassment of caregivers. Coordinate sterilization & vaccination drives.

All animals should be sterilized under ABC program and vaccinated against rabies.

Any act of cruelty, poisoning, confinement is strictly prohibited and punishable under law.

7️⃣ RESIDENT SAFETY

Don't provoke animals. Avoid sudden movements. Report injured animals immediately.

8️⃣ CONFLICT RESOLUTION

Via managing committee → municipal authorities. No individual unlawful action allowed.

9️⃣ WATER & CARE

Provide water bowls. Doesn't obstruct pathways. Shade during hot seasons.

🔟 NO BLANKET BANS

Societies cannot ban feeding community animals. Such actions violate legal provisions.

🩺 Recognizing Common Stray Dog Diseases

Early detection saves lives. Learn to recognize signs and know when to call a vet or rescue organization. Most symptoms appear gradually — observe daily changes.

🔴 MANGE (Skin Mites)

🚨 SIGNS TO LOOK FOR:

• Crusty, scaly patches (usually ears, elbows, legs) • Excessive scratching & biting • Hair loss & baldness • Red, inflamed skin • Foul smell • Sometimes bleeding from scratching

⏱️ TIMELINE:

Develops gradually over 2-4 weeks. Gets worse if untreated.

✓ WHAT TO DO:

1. Call vet immediately (treatable with oral medicine ₹300-800) 2. Isolate from other dogs 3. Avoid touching directly (contagious) 4. Wear gloves when handling

🪱 WORMS & PARASITES

🚨 SIGNS TO LOOK FOR:

• Pot-belly appearance (swollen abdomen) • Diarrhea or loose stools • Weight loss despite eating • Visible worms in stool • Anemia (pale gums) • Lethargy & weakness • Sometimes vomiting

⏱️ TIMELINE:

Can develop at any time. Common in stray pups.

✓ WHAT TO DO:

1. Deworming tablet (₹30-80 from vet or chemist) 2. Most curable at home 3. Repeat dose after 2 weeks 4. Improve nutrition & feed boiled chicken

🩹 SKIN INFECTIONS (Fungal/Bacterial)

🚨 SIGNS TO LOOK FOR:

• Oozing wounds or pus • Red, inflamed patches • Circular lesions (ringworm) • Crusty sores • Yellow discharge • Itching & scratch marks • Bad smell from affected area

⏱️ TIMELINE:

Can spread quickly if untreated. Contagious to other dogs.

✓ WHAT TO DO:

1. Keep wound clean & dry 2. Use Intas D Mag wound spray (₹80-150) 3. Apply Woundheal powder 4. See vet for oral antibiotics if severe 5. Isolate from other dogs

👂 EAR INFECTIONS

🚨 SIGNS TO LOOK FOR:

• Head tilting to one side • Excessive scratching at ears • Foul smell from ears • Yellow/brown discharge • Swollen ears • Sensitivity to touch • Sometimes fever

⏱️ TIMELINE:

Develops quickly, gets painful fast.

✓ WHAT TO DO:

1. See vet soon (₹300-500 for treatment) 2. Do NOT put water in ears 3. Keep dry 4. Vet will provide ear drops (most effective) 5. Clean outer ear gently with cotton

🫁 COUGH, COLD & RESPIRATORY ISSUES

🚨 SIGNS TO LOOK FOR:

• Persistent dry or wet cough • Sneezing • Nasal discharge (clear/yellow) • Difficulty breathing • Lethargy • Loss of appetite • Sometimes fever

⏱️ TIMELINE:

Can last days to weeks. Can turn serious (pneumonia) if ignored.

✓ WHAT TO DO:

1. Monitor for 2-3 days (mild colds can resolve) 2. Keep warm & dry 3. Feed nutritious food 4. If worsens, see vet (antibiotics ₹300-800) 5. Keep away from cold/rain

💩 DIARRHEA & DIGESTIVE ISSUES

🚨 SIGNS TO LOOK FOR:

• Loose or watery stools • Mucus in stool • Blood in stool (serious) • Frequent bowel movements • Straining • Vomiting • Loss of appetite • Dehydration (sunken eyes)

⏱️ TIMELINE:

Can lead to dehydration quickly in puppies. Urgent if bloody.

✓ WHAT TO DO:

1. Stop solid food for 24 hrs 2. Give boiled rice + boiled chicken 3. Increase water intake 4. If bloody or persists >2 days, see vet (₹200-500) 5. Avoid fatty/spicy food

🩸 INJURIES & WOUNDS

🚨 SIGNS TO LOOK FOR:

• Bleeding • Limping or not putting weight on leg • Swelling • Open cuts • Puncture wounds (bites, stabs) • Torn ear/lip • Unable to move normally

⏱️ URGENCY:

Heavy bleeding → Call rescue immediately. Deep punctures → Vet within hours.

✓ WHAT TO DO:

1. Stop heavy bleeding with cloth pressure 2. Clean with water 3. Apply Intas D Mag spray or Woundheal 4. Keep clean & dry 5. See vet if deep/large 6. Watch for infection (swelling, pus)

🪰 MAGGOT INFESTATION

🚨 SIGNS TO LOOK FOR:

• Visible white/cream colored worms in wound • Foul stench • Wound deteriorating rapidly • Pus & blood • Animal appears in severe pain • May occur in weeks-old wounds

⏱️ URGENCY:

✓ WHAT TO DO:

1. Do NOT attempt to remove yourself 2. Call vet/rescue ASAP (₹500-1500 treatment) 3. Vet will flush & remove maggots 4. Requires antibiotics & wound care 5. Prevention: Keep old wounds clean & covered

☠️ POISONING (Chemical/Pesticide/Rat Poison)

🚨 SIGNS TO LOOK FOR:

• Sudden vomiting/diarrhea (sometimes bloody) • Excessive drooling • Trembling/seizures • Difficulty breathing • Lethargy or collapse • Bleeding from mouth/nose • Sudden behavior change

⏱️ URGENCY:

🚨 EMERGENCY - CALL VET IMMEDIATELY (Poison = Life-threatening)

✓ WHAT TO DO:

1. Call vet/rescue NOW (Don't delay) 2. If knew what poison → tell vet 3. Keep animal warm & calm 4. Don't force vomiting (can cause more damage) 5. Vet may give activated charcoal (₹50-100 at home) within 1-2 hrs

💡 IMPORTANT GUIDELINES:

• Not all diseases are fatal. Many are treatable at home with basic care. • Early detection is key. The sooner you notice, the better the outcome. • When in doubt, call a vet. Better safe than sorry. • Prevention is easier than cure. Keep feeding areas clean, watch for changes daily. • Sterilization + vaccination = healthy community. Reduces 80% of diseases.
